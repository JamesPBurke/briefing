## TV / streaming movie recommendations

- The Haunting of Hill House
  - Emotionally devastating horror about family trauma and supernatural evil - a 5★ favorite that fits your taste for psychologically rich, character-driven horror.

- The Babadook
  - Grief and motherhood metaphor horror that balances scares with deep emotional resonance - another 5★ watch that shows your appetite for horror with substance.

- The Substance
  - Elevated body horror with feminist themes and visceral impact - your 4.5★ rating shows you appreciate smart, concept-driven genre fare.

- From
  - Mysterious horror about people trapped in a nightmarish town with monsters that come out at night - psychological terror with slow-burn dread that matches your love for atmospheric, thoughtful scares.

- Servant
  - M. Night Shyamalan's psychological thriller about a mysterious nanny in Philadelphia - religious imagery, unreliable reality, and slow-building tension fit your taste for elevated, weird horror.

- The Midnight Gospel
  - Duncan Trussell's animated blend of reality, metaphysics, and comedy - weird, philosophical, and emotionally resonant, perfect for your appreciation of high-concept storytelling.

- Yellowjackets
  - Survival horror with psychological trauma, mystery, and teenage drama - combines emotional depth with genre thrills in a way that fits your Letterboxd taste.

- Archive 81
  - Found footage horror about an archivist restoring damaged VHS tapes - analog horror vibe, mystery, and emotional core that speaks to your love for smart, atmospheric genre.

- The Leftovers
  - Damon Lindelof's profound exploration of grief, mystery, and meaning after 2% of the world's population vanishes - deeply emotional, philosophical, and character-driven.

- Dark
  - German time-travel conspiracy thriller with complex family secrets and atmospheric dread - intricate plotting and emotional resonance match your taste for smart, layered storytelling.

- Counterpart
  - Espionage + parallel-world sci-fi + adult competence - probably the single cleanest “James show you may have missed.”

- Person of Interest
  - Starts procedural and then quietly becomes one of the smartest AI / surveillance shows on television.

- Bad Sisters
  - Darkly funny, well-observed, and ensemble-driven in a way that plausibly overlaps with Hacks / Derry Girls / Mare of Easttown energy.

- Detectorists
  - Gentle, funny, humane, and specific — a softer shared-watch possibility.

- Somebody Somewhere
  - A warmer character-driven recommendation if the shared overlap leans more Bear / Good Place than pure detective work.

- Scavengers Reign
  - Animated speculative sci-fi with strong worldbuilding and a weirder imagination than most prestige SF.

- Sugar
  - Noir detective structure with a genre wrinkle and a sensibility that feels adjacent to your crime/strangeness overlap.

- The Lazarus Project
  - Time-loop espionage, moral pressure, and speculative plotting — very plausible fit.

- Bodies
  - Time-spanning conspiracy/mystery structure that fits your taste for long-arc puzzle storytelling.
